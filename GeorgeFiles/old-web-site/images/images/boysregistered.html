    AN IMPORTANT EXPLANATORY NOTE ABOUT WAITING LISTS AND DIVISIONAL EXPANSIONS

    At the start of the Registration Process, we allocate 8 spots for each of the twelve (12) U-13 to U-18 Divisions (6 Ice Piles and 6 Snow Banks). That uses up 96 of the maximum number of 180 teams that our local governing body, the Eastern Ontario District Soccer Association will allow us to host in the Boys' Icebreaker. That leaves us with 84 spots that can be allocated to any U-13 to U-18 Division. As demand for any particular Division exceeds 8 teams, we start a Waiting List. However, in order for us to expand a Division and in order to allow the Dual Shoot-Out format that we have followed for over fifteen years, to continue to operate as smoothly as possible, we need to expand any particular Division by four teams at a time. Once we get to four teams on a Waiting List, and as long as an expansion will not cause us to exceed the 180 team limitation, we expand that Division and admit four more teams into the competition.

    The Waiting List is also used as the order in which teams will be admitted into the tournament if an already-accepted team withdraws - which happens about six times each year.

    Those are two good reasons why it is a very good idea to submit an entry application even if a Waiting List is being compiled.

    The same Waiting List practice is applied in the competitive U-9, U-10, U-11 age groups - with the only difference being that we are limited to a total of 144 teams in those four age levels.

          BOYS ICE PILE

          The U-9, U-10, U-11 and U-12 Festivals are for Long-Term Player Development (LTPD) Programme Teams with better skilled or more experienced players.

          The U-13 to U-18 Divisions are for:
          OSA Level #3 teams,
          Quebec "AA" Level teams
          (and Ontario Level #1 and Quebec "AAA" teams that are "playing up" by a year).

          More Specifically: Any team playing at the highest level within an Ontario Regional or a Multi-Jurisdictional league or a Quebec-based league, and/or
          Teams playing at the highest level within their own League.

          Ontario Level #4 and Quebec "A" Teams that qualify to play in the Snow Bank but which ask to play in the Ice Pile may be allowed to play in the Ice Pile.

          OYSL, LSEQ and Super-Y teams must play in the Ice Pile AND in an age group that is one year older than the age group in which they are registered for play in their outdoor League.

          All teams must play in an outdoor League this coming summer.

          BOYS SNOW BANK

          The U-9, U-10, U-11 and U-12 Festivals are for Long-Term Player Development (LTPD) Programme Teams with lesser skilled or less experienced players.

          The U-13 to U-18 Snow Banks include:
          OSA Level #4 and Level #5 teams,
          Quebec "A" Level teams,
          Club Teams playing in an Ontario District League,
          Recreation level teams that are "carded" to play "Competitively", and
          Ontario Level #3 and Quebec "AA" Ice Pile-qualified teams that are "playing up" by at least one year.

          Ontario Level #4 and Quebec "A" Teams that qualify to play in the Snow Bank may ask to play in the Ice Pile.

          All teams must play in an outdoor League this coming summer.
