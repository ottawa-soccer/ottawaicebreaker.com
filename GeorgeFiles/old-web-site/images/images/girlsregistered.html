    AN IMPORTANT EXPLANATORY NOTE ABOUT WAITING LISTS AND DIVISIONAL EXPANSIONS

    At the start of the Registration Process, we allocate 8 spots for each of the twelve (12) U-13 to U-18 Divisions (6 Ice Piles and 6 Snow Banks). That uses up 96 of the maximum number of 180 teams that our local governing body, the Eastern Ontario District Soccer Association, will allow us to host in the Girls' Icebreaker. That leaves us with 84 spots that can be allocated to any U-13 to U-18 Division. As demand for any particular Division exceeds 8 teams, we start a Waiting List. However, in order for us to expand a Division and in order to allow the Dual Shoot-Out format that we have been following for over fifteen years to continue to operate as smoothly as possible, we need to expand any particular Division by four teams at a time. Once we get to four teams on a Waiting List, and as long as an expansion will not cause us to exceed the 180 team limitation, we expand that Division and admit four more teams into the competition.

    The Waiting List is also used as the order in which teams will be admitted into the tournament if an already-accepted team withdraws from the tournament - and that happens about six times a year.

    Those are two very good reasons why it is wise to submit an entry application even if a Waiting List exists.

    However, in a typical year, we usually have more than 84 expansion spots available and that is because the U-16, U-17 and U-18 Divisions often do not fill up; and so those Divisions are often combined, eliminated or shrunk and their unused spots are then allocated to younger age groups.

    The same Waiting List practice is applied to the Festival age groups (U-9, U-10, U-11 and U-12) - with the only difference being that we are limited to a maximum population of 164 teams in those four age groups.

              GIRLS ICE PILE

              The U-9, U-10, U-11 and U-12 Festivals are for Long-Term Player Development (LTPD) Programme Teams with better skilled or more experienced players.

              The U-13 to U-18 Divisions are for:
              OSA Level #3 teams,
              Quebec "AA" Level teams
              (and Ontario Level #1 and Quebec "AAA" teams that are "playing up" by a year).

              More Specifically: Any team playing at the highest level within an Ontario Regional or a Multi-Jurisdictional league or a Quebec-based league, and/or Teams playing at the highest level within their own League.

              Ontario Level #4 and Quebec "A" Teams that qualify to play in the Snow Bank but which ask to play in the Ice Pile may be allowed to play in the Ice Pile.

              OYSL, LSEQ and Super-Y teams must play in the Ice Pile AND in an age group that is one year older than the age group in which they are registered for play in their outdoor League.

              All teams must play in an outdoor League this coming summer.
